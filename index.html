Who I Am

Hello! My name is Amethyst Baumgartner. I am currently a junior at Lick Wilmerding High School. As a 17 year old girl, I beleive I bring a unique perspective to computer science. It can seem difficult or daunting to get involved in CS, especially as a middle or high school girl. Looking back to 8th grade, I remember wanting to learn more about coding and technology, but struggling because I wasn't sure which resources were available. I found online resources, but didnt know where to start. Two years later, I was able to take my first computer science class. After enjoying the experience, I continued to pursue my passion by participating in Girls Who Code, a 7 week computer science immersion program. It was an amazing experience-I learned how to code, met inspiring women, and made great friends. Girls Who Code continued to provide me with more awesome experiences, as I competed in a hackathon and won first place with four other female hackers. At my high school, I chose to do an independent study on the topic of women in computer science. This website is the final product of my research. I hope to provide information about the lack of women in CS, stories from real women computer scientists, and provide a resources that girls can use to get invovled in CS.

The Issue

0.4%. 14%. 25%

What do these numbers represent? Only 0.4% girls select computer science as a college major. Just 14% of women are awarded a degree in computer science. And merely 25% of women have positions in the computing and technical fields. The numbers speak for themselves. It clear that there is a major gender gap in the field of computer science. Women are severely underrepresented in a field dominated by their male counterparts. But why? There a many factors that contribute the lack of women in computer science. Traditionally, society has labled coding and computers as a "boy thing" even though the first code was written by females. Furthermore, stigma and sterotypes that surround girls being involved in computer science deter many girls from even trying computer science. Another obstacle that prevents girls from participating in CS is due to the "unknown." Because there is a lack of information surrounding computer science, many girls avoid computer science simply becuase they don't know enough about it. This website aims to combat this problem by providing a comprehensive collection of resources and how to use them!

Real Women Real Stories

The following are quotes from women computer scientists who I interviewed. They range from interns to senior engineers.

Dina Westland: SQUARE software engineer

"There is a major shortage of tech workers in the US and we are losing out on almost half of our workforce dut to the lack of women in computing. It is very important that women get involved so that we don't fall behind."

Naomi Boyd: Senior Program Manager at Dolby

"I think girls are afraid that they are not smart enough to get involved in computer science and I absolutely believe that is false. Once they get involved in computer science, it is not as difficult or scary as anything else they don't know much about and will soon find themselves with a wider and more exciting [range] of opportunities than if they were not involved in something technical."

Nupur Garg: Student at Cal Poly, Intern at Square

"I recommend that girls should just dive in. They should take all the opportunities they get to start learning. Don't let someone tell you that you cannot do it because of who you are. Don't hold off on opportunities targeted towards minorities because you feel it is not fair that you get the advantage. These programs are in place for a reason-to help level the playing field of computer science. Take these opportunities, find mentors who know what you are going through, and know that you can do it."

Resources: Summer Programs

Girls Who Code Summer Program is a 7 week long computer science immersion program. You will learn the basics of several programming language such as Scratch, Python, and JavaScript. You will also learn animation and robotics! Additionally, you will have opportunities to meet inspiring mentors and other prominent figures in the computer science field. Applications come out in January.

Black girls code introduces young girls of color to programming and new technology. They strive to form a generation of coders who will become builders of technological innovation and of their own futures. Applications ongoing.

A week long immersion program led by Square engineers that introduces dynamic and diverse high school students to careers in engineering and technology. Code camp applications will open in late January/early February.

All girls summer program for ages 10-15, offering creative courses in coding, web design and more.

Resources: During the Year

A program that teaches computer science to 6-12th grade girls after school. Learn basic skills in Scratch, Python and JavaScript; go on fun field trips!

After school program for 1st-8th graders to teach kids to code in Marin and San Francisco. Scratch, Java Script, HTML, Unity and more. Enrollment is on going.

Global technology entrepreneurship competition for girls ages 10-18. Spend 12 weeks designing and building an app! Technovation 2016 is open for registration.

Provides a long-term community for female technologists. Apply for the NCWIT Aspirations in computing award. Applications close November 1.
